The politics of New Zealand function within a framework of a unitary parliamentary representative democracy.
New Zealand is a constitutional monarchy in which a hereditary monarch, Queen Elizabeth II - is the sovereign and head of state.
Executive power in New Zealand is based on the principle that "The Queen reigns, but the government rules".
The office of prime minister is, in practice, the most powerful political office in New Zealand.
Government ministers are selected from the elected members of the New Zealand Parliament.
